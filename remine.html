ELECTRONIC WASTE

E-waste or electronic waste is created when an electronic product is discarded after the end of its useful life. The rapid expansion of technology and the consumption driven society results in the creation of a very large amount of e-waste.

ELECTRONIC WASTE CLASSIFICATION

To categorize the electronic products, it is necessary to group them into sensible and practical categories. Classification of the products may even help to determine the process to be used for disposal of the product. Making the classifications, in general, is helping to describe e-waste. Classifications has not defined special details. On the other hand, classifications should not be too aggregated because of countries differences in interpretation.

LITHIUM-ION BATTERY RECYCLING TECHNOLOGY

A combi­nation of mechanical and hydromet­al­lur­gical technologies are used to recycle the battery materials. Our indus­trial-scale, low-CO2 processes allow us to recover lithium, cobalt, manganese and nickel from the battery for reuse in the production of new batteries. Our optimised battery treatment processes are safe, sustainable, and efficient.

We are a e-waste recycler who is the basic level in India.

We believe in the sustainable development of the country and the world and works to accomplish it.

---What we does?---

->Remine is a small start up e-wasye recycling plant which collects the electronic waste from the consumers and are transferred to the recycling process. It includes many stages such as segregation, dismantling, treatment and disposal.

->We also recycle the lithium batteries allowing the users to recover minerals such as lithium, cobalt, manganese from the battery and reuse them in the production of new batteries. These treatment processes are safe, sustainable, and efficient.
